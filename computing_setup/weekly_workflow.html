üìö Before Each Lab

Every week, new lab materials get added to the materials repository. Here‚Äôs how to get them:

  1. Open the materials project
    • Navigate to your MA22019/materials/ folder

  3. Check for new content

üß™ During the Lab

  1. Navigate to labs/lab_XX/
  2. Double-click lab_XX.Rproj to open that specific lab

  1. Work through the exercises
  2. Save frequently (Ctrl/Cmd + S) ‚Äî there‚Äôs no auto-save!

Homework Workflow

Homeworks are formative (for learning, not your final grade). You‚Äôll get feedback from your Tutor to help you improve. Here‚Äôs the full process:

Step 1: Clone Your Personal Homework Repo

Each homework has a unique URL just for you.

  1. Find your URL on Moodle
    • It looks like: https://github.bath.ac.uk/ma22019-2026/hw-01-yourusername.git
  2. Clone in RStudio
    • Go to File ‚Üí New Project ‚Üí Version Control ‚Üí Git
    • Paste your URL
    • Leave ‚ÄòProject directory name‚Äô blank
    • Important! Click Browse and navigate to MA22019/homework/
    • Check the box ‚ÄòOpen in new session‚Äô
    • Click Create Project
WarningClone to the Right Folder!

Always clone homework into MA22019/homework/ and each coursework into MA22019/coursework/. This keeps everything organized and makes it easy to find your work later.

Note: For a refresh on file paths and creating folders, see the Setup Guide or check the Quick Reference.

Step 2: Do the Work

  1. Open the .qmd file provided
  2. Work through the questions
  3. Click Render regularly to preview your work as Markdown
  4. Save frequently! (Ctrl/Cmd + S)
TipPreviewing Your Work

Every time you click Render, a Markdown (.md) file is created on your computer. RStudio automatically opens the preview so you can see exactly how your finished report looks. This is how you check your work before submitting!

The .md file stays on your computer ‚Äî it won‚Äôt be explicitly uploaded to GitHub (unless you push). You only submit your source files (.qmd, .R), and your Tutor or Lecturer will render them to check your output.

Step 3: Commit Your Progress

Think of commits as ‚Äúsave points‚Äù in a video game. You want several of these!

  1. Go to the Git pane (top-right)
  2. Check the boxes next to files you changed (this is called ‚Äústaging‚Äù)
  3. Click Commit
  4. Write a brief message describing what you did (e.g., ‚ÄúCompleted Q1 and Q2‚Äù)
  5. Click Commit again
TipCommit Early, Commit Often!

Aim for at least 3 commits per homework. This shows your progress and protects your work. If something breaks, you can always go back to a previous commit!

Step 4: Push Your Work (Submit!)

  1. Click the Push button (green up arrow ‚¨ÜÔ∏è)
  2. Enter your Bath username and password if prompted
  3. Done! Your work is now safely on GitHub üéâ
NoteWhat Gets Submitted?

When you push, everything in your folder is uploaded: your .qmd source key, your data, AND your rendered .md file.

Why submit both?

  • .qmd (The Recipe): This lets Tutors check your code, reproduce your analysis, and give detailed feedback.
  • .md (The Cake): This lets Tutors instantly see your plotted graphs and tables on GitHub. Since it‚Äôs Markdown, GitHub renders it perfectly!

Always Render before pushing! If you change your code but don‚Äôt Render, your .md file will be ‚Äústale‚Äù (outdated). Make sure they match!

Once pushed, your Tutor can see your code and provide feedback directly on your repository (via the Issues tab). This is the primary way we help you improve!

Step 5: Verify Your Submission

Trust but verify! Make sure your work actually uploaded:

  1. Go to github.bath.ac.uk in your browser
  2. Find your homework repository
  3. Check that:
    • Your .qmd, .md, and other files are there
    • The commit time is before the deadline

Coursework Workflow

Coursework is summative ‚Äî it counts toward your final mark! The process is exactly the same as homework, but with one key difference: clone into the coursework/ folder instead.

ImportantCoursework Submission Reminder

Always click Render before your final push to make sure your code actually runs without errors!

The 3-Commit Rule üìú

Common Questions (FAQ)

Q: I forgot to pull before lab. Am I in trouble?

Nope! Just pull now ‚Äî you‚Äôll get everything immediately. No harm done.

Q: I can‚Äôt find my homework/coursework repo URL.

Check the MA22019 Moodle page for the URL. On Rstudio make sure you‚Äôre logged into github.bath.ac.uk (not github.com).

Q: I cloned to the wrong folder. Help!

Easy fix: delete the incorrectly-placed folder and clone again to the right location.

Q: How do I know my work was actually submitted?

Visit github.bath.ac.uk, find your repo, and check that your latest files and commit time are visible. If you can see it there, we can see it too!

Q: RStudio is asking for my password every time I push.

That‚Äôs normal on university computers. Just enter your Bath credentials each time.

Important: If you have enabled Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on your GitHub account, your regular password will NOT work. You must use a Personal Access Token (PAT) instead. See the Troubleshooting Guide for instructions.

On your personal laptop, you can set up credential caching to avoid this.
